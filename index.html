Foundation
DME & Medical Insurance — The Basics
What we do, who we work with, and why Medicare Part B is our focus.
What is DME?
Durable Medical Equipment — reusable devices prescribed for home use. Braces, wheelchairs, oxygen equipment, CPAP machines.
Medicare Eligibility
Federal health insurance for US citizens. Eligible if age 65+ OR under 65 with qualifying disability. Must be US citizen or permanent resident.
Our Role
Telemarketing agency. We generate and qualify complete leads, then hand them to suppliers. We do not supply the equipment.
Key Focus — Medicare Part B: Covers doctor visits, outpatient care, and DME (braces). Parts A, C, and D are not relevant to our process.
Insurance Types We Skip
PPO, HMO, MSP, Medicaid-only, and Medicare Advantage (Part C). If any of these is the patient's primary — lead is not eligible.
What Makes a Good Lead?
Active Medicare Part B · patient alive · no recent hospital/nursing home stay · no private plan as primary · valid PECOS-enrolled doctor with individual NPI.
Lead Structure
Required Information — Patient & Doctor
A lead = PT info + Doctor info. Both parts are mandatory. No exceptions.
Patient (PT) Info
1
Full Name + DOB
First & last name. Date of birth used for identity confirmation on every call.
2
MBI / MCN (Medicare ID)
11 alphanumeric characters. Example: 1EG4-TE5-MK73. Required for billing.
3
Physical Address
Must be a real street address. No PO Boxes. State & zip must match client criteria.
4
Measurements + Braces
Height, weight, waist. Shoe size only for ankle braces (BAB). Use exact brace codes.
Doctor Info
1
Full Name + Phone + Fax
Doctor's first & last name. Direct fax number is critical — prior auth paperwork goes here.
2
NPI Number (Individual)
10-digit National Provider Identifier. Must be Individual NPI — not Organizational. Example: 1234567893.
3
PECOS Enrollment
Must be enrolled in PECOS. Verify via Medicare NPI Registry. Not enrolled = claim denied.
4
Active State License
License must be valid and match the practice state. Expired or out-of-state = claim denial.
Organizational NPI is NOT accepted. Always verify the NPI belongs to the specific individual physician, not the clinic or hospital group.

Call Scripts
First Call — Confirming Mutuality
Speak slowly. Never leave an awkward silence. One second pause after the intro line.
Opening
Opening — Speak slowly, pause 1 second after intro "Hello, this is [Your Name] calling from Liberty Medical Supplies. I'm reaching out in regards to the mutual patient between us and Dr. [Doctor's Name]." (1-second pause)

"The name of the patient is [Patient's Name] and the date of birth is [Patient's DOB]."
After Confirming Patient is Active
After Confirmation — Get the fax number "Okay, so may I have the best fax number for the doctor? We need to send a prior authorization request for the patient that we need to be signed."
Closing — Based on GK Attitude
If GK Was Friendly "So by any chance, would the doctor be able to sign the paperwork by the end of the day?"
If GK Wasn't Super Nice "So roughly speaking, how long would it take for the provider to sign the paperwork?"
First Follow-Up
Follow-Up Call "Hi, this is [Your Name] calling from Liberty Medical Supplies. I'm reaching out to follow up on a fax we sent on [Date Fax Was Sent], and I'm just making sure it's been received successfully from your side."

Then confirm: patient name, DOB, and purpose of the request — same as before.
Voicemail
Voicemail Scripts
Use voicemail only as a last option. Follow the steps after leaving the message.
First Call Voicemail
First Call VM — Use only when no one answers "Hi, my name is [Your Name] from Liberty Medical Supplies. I'm reaching out in regards to a mutual patient. The patient's name is [Patient's Name], and their date of birth is [DOB]. I need to check if this patient is a current patient with Dr. [Doctor's Name]. Please call me as soon as you can, as it's urgent. My callback number is (331) 800-4684."
Follow-Up Voicemail
Follow-Up VM — Include fax number and request "Hi, my name is [Your Name] from Liberty Medical Supplies. I'm reaching out regarding a fax I sent to Dr. [Doctor's Name] for a mutual patient. The patient's name is [Patient's Name], and their date of birth is [DOB]. If you received it, I kindly request the provider's signature along with the most recent office notes, faxed back to us at (888) 627-0067. For questions, please call me at (331) 800-4684. Thank you, and have a blessed day."
Critical VM Steps: (1) Leave message clearly. (2) Wait for IVR/automated system to finish. (3) Press the required number to save VM. (4) Only hang up after completing step 3 — hanging up early = VM not received.
Rebuttals
Common Objections & How to Handle Them
Stay professional. Redirect, don't argue. Always aim to get the fax number.
Q
"Who are you with again?"
"With Liberty Medical Supplies. This call is regarding a prior authorization request we need to send to the doctor."
Q
"What medical supplies are you requesting?"
"The order was placed for [requested braces]." — Then if asked who placed it: "It was placed by Mr./Mrs. [Patient's Name]."
Q
"The doctor wouldn't be able to authorize such an order."
"It's totally fine — we're not seeking a signature. We're looking for the supervision of the doctor on the paperwork, regardless of what it is."
Q
"We cannot confirm whether the patient is active."
"Totally fine. We're just making sure the prior authorization is addressed correctly to the right physician. But anyways — can you provide me with the best fax number?"
Q
"Who ordered the brace?"
"The patient requested the brace and was referred to their doctor for evaluation. A medical provider must sign before anything can be dispensed, per HIPAA requirements."
Q
"How did the patient reach you / did you call the patient?"
"According to our system, the patient contacted us regarding this order. We are a third-party provider — orders are placed by the patient, usually under the guidance of their PCP or another doctor."
Common Cases — Part 1
Real Situations & Best Responses
Scenarios you'll face daily — handle them with confidence.
!
Doctor is on vacation.
  • Ask if a covering doctor or PA can sign instead.
  • If not, note when the doctor is back and chase another doctor in the meantime.
!
Doctor treats area X, but brace is for area Y.
  • Explain the patient requested braces for both — ask if they can sign for the area they treat.
  • Always verify before faxing if the doctor covers the specific area.
!
Fax sent but no response after multiple follow-ups.
  • Try medical records or nurse directly. Leave a message noting urgency.
  • Follow up for 2–3 weeks. If still nothing → escalate, look for another doctor.
  • Stress urgency politely — ask them to remind the doctor to sign and return.
!
Patient not active with this doctor — no other doctors listed.
  • Ask GK if patient was referred elsewhere.
  • Call the patient directly — introduce as calling from a doctor's office, not a supply company.
!
Office says they didn't receive the fax.
  • Ask for an alternative fax or email. Try other departments.
  • Offer to resend while they're on the line.
Common Cases — Part 2
More Scenarios & Solutions
Edge cases that require extra care — stay consistent.
!
GK says there's no mention of a brace in the chart.
  • Explain the patient requested it recently — may not have been discussed in prior visits.
  • Ask if the provider can evaluate the patient and add a diagnosis. Offer to resend paperwork.
!
GK says patient didn't request the brace.
  • Agree they can confirm with the patient. Once confirmed, offer to resend paperwork.
  • Leave the lead 2 days, call back trying a different GK. Pursue other doctors in parallel.
!
Doctor signed but chart notes are missing.
  • Contact medical records directly — state you have a signed order and need supporting docs.
  • If needed, call another doctor and explain the situation to obtain compliant documentation.
!
GK refuses to provide any info — including fax number.
  • Call back later and try a different GK or doctor directly.
  • Search the fax number online for that office.
  • Call back asking only for the fax, without a full intro.
!
Patient is now seeing a PA — not the original doctor.
  • If form already sent: ask office to forward it to the PA. Note their name + NPI.
  • If form not sent yet: request the PA's fax number and send directly.
!
GK says paperwork must go to PCP or specialist.
  • Ask for the PCP's name and send directly to them.
  • If PCP denied it, explain the patient referred you to this doctor as part of their treatment plan.
Pre-Chase Checklist
Data Validation Before Calling
Run every lead through these checks before contacting the doctor's office.
Patient Checks
  • Patient is alive — confirmed active
  • Active Medicare Part B — not just Part A
  • No recent hospital or nursing home stay
  • No primary PPO, HMO, or MSP coverage
  • S&S matches requested braces
  • State & zip match client criteria
  • Physical street address — no PO Box
  • DOB is consistent with eligibility
Doctor Checks
  • PECOS enrolled — verified via NPI Registry
  • NPI is Individual — not Organizational
  • Specialty appropriate for requested braces
  • Active license in the correct state
  • License not expired or out-of-state
  • Clinic hours match call/fax schedule
Once all checks pass and the lead is marked "Good & Eligible" → proceed to Active Chasing Phase. Contact the doctor's office by phone and/or fax to obtain physician signature.
NPI Registry
Use to verify PECOS enrollment quickly. Also confirms individual vs. organizational provider type.
State Medical Board
For direct validation of doctor license status, expiration date, and state authorization.
CRM / Internal DB
Track provider license status, flag non-compliant doctors, and maintain detailed clinic information.
Workflow
The Full Chasing Process — Step by Step
From receiving a lead to getting a signed order. Every step matters.
1
Receive Lead + Data Validation
Check all PT and doctor fields. Run PECOS, NPI, S&S, and eligibility checks. Mark lead as Good or flag issues before proceeding.
2
First Call — Confirm Mutuality
Call the doctor's office. Confirm the patient is active. Introduce as Liberty Medical Supplies. Get the fax number. Ask about signing timeline.
3
Send the Fax (Prior Auth Request)
Fax the prior authorization paperwork to the confirmed fax number. Log the exact date and time of transmission.
4
Follow-Up Calls (Every 1–2 Days)
Check if fax was received. Ask for signature status. If no response after 2–3 weeks → escalate. Try other departments or another doctor.
5
Obtain Signed DO + Chart Notes
Doctor's Order must be signed. Most cases also require recent office/chart notes showing medical necessity. Request from medical records if needed.
6
Submit to Processing
Signed DO + chart notes received → file moves to Processing for final submission to the supplier and Medicare billing.
